The Z3 result is therefore mixed rather than a uniform timing victory.
Euf-viper wins most pairings and the geometric metric, but its common-solve
time is 7,315.49s versus Z3's 5,075.91s. Timeout charging reverses that deficit
because euf-viper solves two additional instances.

Yices2 remains the decisive target: it solves the complete corpus, wins 7,037
of 7,502 common pairings, and is about `4.27x` faster by full timeout-charged
total. Euf-viper's only remaining timeout is
`QF_UF_sokoban.2.prop1_ab_br_max.smt2`.

## Soundness Boundary

The measured binary is known to return SAT incorrectly on the separate
Boolean-as-data pigeonhole fixture. The official corpus contains no observed
mismatch, so these timings remain valid for these 7,503 files. They are not a
general solver-correctness or superiority result.

The exact accepted-lineage repair is branch `soundness/accepted-58efe`, commit
`53c12f7`. It passes 91 all-feature tests and the counterexample through auto,
Varisat, CaDiCaL, and CaDiCaL-refine locally. It must pass WMI differential and
paired corpus gates before replacing this performance checkpoint.

## Decision

The Z3 coverage frontier is reached on this corpus, but the common hard tail
still needs repair. Yices2 cannot be reached by converting one or two timeout
cases; the campaign needs broad head acceleration plus finite and graph-tail
proof-system changes. No global claim is allowed until the repaired binary is
measured and independently validated.
